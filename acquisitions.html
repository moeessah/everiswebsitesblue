01 Criteria

What we look for.
What we don't.

What fits

  • Apps or SaaS products with paying users and real retention
  • Annual recurring revenue between $500K and $2M
  • Profitable, or close enough that a small operational push gets you there
  • A clear use case customers understand and pay for
  • Clean financials and a cap table that will not slow things down
  • Founders who want the product to outlast them

What doesn't

  • Pre-revenue products, no matter how promising the idea
  • Turnarounds with shrinking users and unclear unit economics
  • Businesses that depend on one platform's goodwill to survive
  • Businesses that need outside capital before they can be sold
  • Anything where the primary asset is the founder's personal brand
  • Services businesses dressed up as software

If you are outside the range, or not sure whether you fit, write to us anyway. We will tell you honestly in the first reply.

02 The process

From first email
to closed.

Short and transparent, run by the two people you started talking to. No committees, no waiting weeks for a reply.

48 hours

First reply and a real conversation

You write. Rob or Liam reads it and replies within two business days. If it is not a fit, we say so in the first message. If it is, we book 30 minutes to actually talk.

1 week

Indicative offer, in writing

After we have looked at the basics, we send a written indicative offer with a valuation range, a deal structure, and the shape of what happens to you after close.

2 weeks

Letter of intent and exclusivity

If the numbers work, we sign an LOI. You stop talking to other buyers. We give you a specific list of what we need for diligence, scoped to what actually matters, not a boilerplate data room checklist.

45 days

Due diligence and close

Diligence, legal, close. We are small, which means fewer committees and faster decisions. Most deals of our size are signed in 6 to 8 weeks from LOI.

03 After the deal closes

You decide
what comes next.

Every founder we talk to is somewhere different. Some want to keep building with more support. Some are ready to hand over the keys. Some want a middle ground. All three are normal answers. None of them change the price.

Option I

Stay and keep building

You continue as the operator. We take the boring, heavy stuff off your plate (finance, hiring pipeline, infrastructure, pricing) so you can focus on the product you came here to build.

Option II

Step into an advisory seat

You move from full-time operator to a part-time advisor for a transition period. We bring in, or promote from within, a new operator. You stay close to the product without the 7am Slack pings.

Option III

Walk away clean

You hand it over and go do whatever is next. Your team stays. The brand stays. We run it. You get on with your life. No earnout that ties you down for two years.

Whichever path you choose, two things do not change. Your brand stays on the product, and your team stays in place unless they choose otherwise. We do not rebrand and we do not consolidate.

04 Frequently asked

The questions
founders actually ask.

01

How do you value the business?

A multiple of annual recurring revenue or seller discretionary earnings, depending on the shape of the business. We can tell you roughly where you land before we see diligence, and we are direct about how we got there. No surprises after the LOI.

02

How is the deal structured?

Cash at close is the default. We can work with modest seller notes or earnouts when it makes sense for both sides, but we do not hide the price behind a complicated structure. The deal sheet fits on one page and you do not need a lawyer to read it before your lawyer reads it.

03

What happens to my team?

They stay. We don't make staffing changes for the sake of it. If your team is good, we want them to stay. If someone wants to move on after the deal, they are free to, and we will help them land somewhere good.

04

Will you change the product?

We invest in it. Pricing, onboarding, retention, hiring: the areas most small SaaS have rebuilt three times and still gotten wrong. We bring what we learned running Time Doctor for 14 years. The product itself stays focused on the customers who already love it.

05

Do you sign NDAs before a first conversation?

Happy to. We prefer a 30 minute intro call first so we are not signing NDAs for businesses that were never going to be a fit, but if you would rather sign one up front, send it and we will come back the same day.

06

Everis is new. Why trust your timeline?

Fair question. Everis is new, but the team is not. Rob and Liam have spent fourteen years running a bootstrapped, fully remote SaaS and know exactly what it feels like to be on the other side of this conversation. We will tell you what we are thinking at every stage, and if anything changes, you will hear it from us directly.
